# Executive Summary

HealthLake CI is designed as a unified healthcare analytics platform built entirely on Microsoft Fabric. The solution combines historical analytics, real-time intelligence, enterprise reporting, and governance within a single architecture.

By separating operational responsibilities across specialized workloads while keeping data centralized in OneLake, the platform supports both day-to-day hospital operations and long-term strategic decision-making.

---

# Solution Overview

The platform manages two complementary types of healthcare data:

### Historical Data

Operational datasets such as hospitals, departments, patients, admissions, and medical resources are stored, transformed, and modeled for business reporting.

### Real-Time Data

Vital sign readings generated by connected medical devices are continuously streamed into the platform, enabling live monitoring and operational alerting.

Together, these data sources provide a complete view of hospital activity.

---

# Architecture Components

| Layer                      | Responsibility                                  |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Real-Time Intelligence** | Capture and analyze live medical events         |
| **Lakehouse**              | Store and transform operational healthcare data |
| **Warehouse**              | Deliver enterprise-ready dimensional models     |
| **Semantic Model**         | Centralize trusted business metrics             |
| **Power BI**               | Provide operational and executive dashboards    |

Each component has a dedicated responsibility while contributing to a unified analytics platform.

---

# Solution Principles

The architecture is built around four key principles:

* Unified data platform.
* Separation of responsibilities.
* Real-time and historical analytics.
* Secure and governed business data.

These principles ensure that the platform remains scalable, maintainable, and ready to support future healthcare initiatives.
